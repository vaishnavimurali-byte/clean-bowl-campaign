The Clean Bowl -
A fictional brand. A real marketing workflow.

The Clean Bowl doesn't exist. I created it as a project to answer a question I've been thinking about for a while: what does an end-to-end AI-assisted marketing campaign actually look like? Using a fictional premium clean-label pet food brand, I documented the campaign across five connected stages, with the final two exploring AI's recommendations for distribution and measurement. Every prompt, every iteration, every tool switch, and every decision is documented here. Not to prove that AI can replace marketers, but to understand where it genuinely helps, where it falls short, and what still depends on human judgment.

Progress

Stages 4 and 5 are where brand context, budget, and history take over. What follows is how I'd think about it for The Clean Bowl - not a prescription, just a starting point.

What I'd keep - and why

Distribution for a premium brand isn't about being everywhere - it's about being in the right places. AI gave me a comprehensive channel list. What it couldn't tell me is that online and offline aren't separate tracks for a brand like this - they reinforce each other. And that selectivity is the strategy, not scale.

I've also seen that curated, intimate events work exceptionally well for luxury products, at least in Bangalore. Getting the food in front of pet parents - and the pets - is the most honest marketing you can do. The product either earns the room or it doesn't.

Quick commerce and paid media come after organic traction, not before. You don't run ads on unvalidated creative.

The 3-6-9 plan - Bangalore first

Months 1-3

Foundation - do one thing well

Launch Instagram. 3x feed/week using Stage 3 assets.
Build D2C website. Ingredient-forward product page.
Set up email welcome flow - 3 emails max.
1-2 intimate events in Bangalore (Koramangala / Indiranagar). 15-20 pet parents, animals present, no hard sell.
Approach 2-3 boutique pet stores for shelf presence.
Gift product to 3-5 micro-influencers. No brief, no contract.

Months 4-6

Build - scale what worked

Double down on the content pillar driving saves and shares.
Apply for HUFT and Supertails listing.
Farmer's and artisanal markets. Sampling-led.
Premium residential communities (Prestige, Brigade) for pet parent events.
Vet clinic rack cards or sampling in 2-3 premium clinics.
If email open rates are healthy, extend the welcome sequence.

Months 7-9

Scale - add reach to what's working

List on quick commerce (Blinkit, Zepto). Brand is established enough now.
Start the email newsletter - there's an audience to send it to.
Pet-friendly cafe tie-up for ongoing brand presence.
Formalise with 1-2 micro-influencers who organically performed.

Beyond 9 months

Consider a second city only after Bangalore is working. Mumbai operates differently - more aspirational, more visibility-driven. A Bandra or Colaba pop-up, HUFT flagship, a premium vet tie-up. Delhi/NCR after that. Don't stretch before one city is proven.

Keep at launch

Save rate

A save means "I want to come back to this." For a considered purchase like switching pet food, that's closer to intent than a like.

Share rate

If someone shares a Clean Bowl post, they're publicly endorsing a value - not just a piece of content. The habit parallel doing its job.

Content performance by pillar

Tells you which pillar is resonating. That shifts where creative effort goes next.

Story replies

One genuine reply is worth 100 views. Replies are community forming.

Email open rate (Email 1 only)

If people don't open the first email, the rest of the sequence doesn't exist.

Skip for now

Raw follower count

500 engaged followers who buy beat 10,000 who scrolled past.

ROAS

No paid media at launch.

Conversion rate

Hard to attribute cleanly at this stage, and The Clean Bowl doesn't exist.

Website traffic from social

Easy to track, hard to interpret without a benchmark.

Story views

A Story reply tells you more than 1,000 views from the wrong audience.
